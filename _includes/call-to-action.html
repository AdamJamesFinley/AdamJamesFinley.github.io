Welcome! This site contains information and materials related to my ongoing research.

I completed my PhD at the University of Exeter in May 2020, as part of the AWESoMeStars ERC project under the supervision of Prof. Sean Matt. Afterwards, I continued working with Prof. Sean Matt in Exeter as a Postdoctoral Research Associate until December 2020. From then onwards, I have been working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at CEA Paris-Saclay with Prof. Sacha Brun, as part of the WHOLESUN ERC project.

My research focuses on the atmospheres of stars like the Sun, which are heated to millions of degrees by magnetohydrodynamic processes and subsequently escape in the form of stellar winds. During my PhD I investigated how these winds remove angular momentum from Sun-like stars using a combination of numerical models and observations. However there remain fundamental challenges in measuring this even for our closest star… Since then, my research has focused more on quantifying the angular momentum loss rate of the Sun, and better understanding the rotation of the Sun’s atmosphere. As part of the WholeSun project, I am also investigating the heating of the solar atmosphere, and the generation of magnetic flux inside Sun-like stars. I am also interested by long-term trendsin the Sun’s behavior, be it the persistence of active longitudes or the role of coronal holes in sculpting structure in the solar wind. Part of my time is also devoted to following the Sun’s activity, and producing weekly bulletins that are widely distributed.
