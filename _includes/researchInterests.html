Research Interests

Stellar Winds

Low-mass stars host hot, million degree, atmospheres which drive a steady stream of plasma from their surface. These winds carry away mass, but also angular momentum which is crucial in explaining the observed rotation periods of stars as they age.

Stellar Magnetism

Low-mass stars generate magnetic fields through an internal dynamo; powered by the interplay of rotation and convection. These magnetic fields energise the outer atmosphere, influence the mass-loss in the stellar wind, and the subsequent angular momentum-loss process.

Numerical Simulation

In order to study the influence of magnetic fields have on angular momentum loss in stellar winds, I perform magnetohydrodynamic simulations using the PLUTO code.

Rotation Evolution

I produce semi-analytic relationships for the braking torque on stars, given their observable properties. These formulae can then be used to model the rotation period evolution of low-mass stars, or in comparison to the torques required by current rotation-evolution models.
